What's Matcha Made out of?

Matcha is a grounded whole green tea leaves using a milestone that has been harvested blocking by the sunrays or sunlight and completing it with Tencha It usually tastes a bit earthy, vegetal (green), savory, a little bitter, and a little sweet.

In Japan, they're typically well-known harvested in region of Uji (Kyoto), Nishio (Aichi) & Yame. Uji, Kyoto considered to be the birthplaced of Matcha, which produce some of the best Green Tea!

The way of Tea

Matcha is a part of crucial role by shaping Japan's Tea Culture, by the tea master Sen no Rikkyu that's still followed today to have a deep connection within Japanese Tea Ceremony that Symbolizes of Hospitality and Respect where it can be held in special occasions as well including weddings & funeral.
